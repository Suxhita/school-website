Our Facilities

our library

a big place to think about small things

To inculcate the spirit of continuous learning among students and enhance knowledge, the campus is equipped with a state-of-the art library. The peaceful and serene atmosphere of the library induces the students to cultivate good reading habits and spend enriching hours in the library undisturbed. Over twenty thousand books, journals, magazines, CD-ROMs and videos from across the world are made available to the students.
The massive library is not just place with a wide collection of books for all ages and groups of students, but also acts as a resource centre for enhancing knowledge. The students are periodically asked to review the books they read, participate in debates and present their views. This process not only inculcates the reading habit in the students but also brings in the value of comprehension of the learning process.




the auditorium

a huge place for huge celebrations

A state of the art auditorium with a seating capacity of over thousand people is located in the campus. Set amidst scenic landscapes and equipped with high-end audio-visual facilities, centralized A/C and good acoustics, the auditorium serves as the ideal venue to host seminars, conferences, exhibitions, workshops, theatre and cultural performances of all kinds. Acclaimed movies both international and national are also screened periodically in the auditorium for the students.







health care facility

the solution to a healthy and happy life is a healthy and happy body

The campus health organization is headed by a resident Medical Officer who with his team ensures the health and hygiene of the TVIS campus. A 20 bed TVIS hospital including dental care is centrally located for dispensing health and handling all hygiene/sanitation issues. The periodic health examination of the staff and students, daily medical OPD and trauma care is dispensed from the campus hospital. Two ambulances on call with stretcher, oxygen and first aid are available 24×7 for any emergencies.






SWIMMING
the kids can easily get the hang of body coordination here

It is a modern aquatic complex with two separate pools. Pool 1 is of 4ft depth, 25 meter length and 15 meter width and is used by senior children. Pool 2 is a shallow pool of one and a half feet depth and is for the children of primary school. The complex has separate washrooms for the boys and girls. It also has a gallery.

MULTI-GYM
great space for kids to train their bodies

STVIS has a full- fledged Gymnasium with the latest equipments for physical conditioning This Air- Conditioned Multi-Purpose Gymnasium will be available for use by the trainees. The Gym has facilities such as Chest Conditioner and Press Statio, Body Jack, imported Ex-Cycles, imported Manual Treadmill, MMS Chromed Dumbbells with Rubber Rings and Rubber Handles of different sizes and weights. The trainer ensures the kind of training required for each individual.

ARTS AND CULTURE
great way for kids to relive thier histories and cultures

At TVIS Campus, as we understand the all round development of value, skills and abilities are imperative for the growth of a student. We identify and nurture talents in the areas of arts, music, dance, quiz, elocution, yoga and various hobbies that bring out the creativity of the student. We identify students who excel in any particular activity and provide additional training to complete at national and International level. We provide ample opportunities and encourage in all artistic endeavors that helps in the personal, social and cultural development of each student. We also organize annual cultural festivals and encourage the students to exhibit their talents. Year after year students look forward to the grand carnival to showcase their talent in their own inimitable style and flavor with an array of programmes. We ensure dignified personalities grace the occasion to boost the confidence of the students.

  • Teacher arrested for abusing boy

    "A 57-year-old teacher was arrested on charges of sexually abusing a seven-year-old student on Monday. Police said the boy, a student of Class III in a private school in Saidapet opened up to an uncle about the abuse he suffered under the accused, Reuben Ravi Kumar, a teacher in the same school.

    The behavior of the accused came to light when the family of the boy noticed a change in the boy’s behavior. “He seemed unusually tired every evening for at least a week. And he did not play during the weekend as he used to,” a close relative said. “We asked him what was wrong but he initially held back his experiences with the teacher. He later opened up to his paternal uncle,” he added.

    The family lodged a complaint with the police on Monday morning. As the news of the abuse spread, several parents rushed to the school and some allegedly even hit the accused teacher. Police rushed to the spot after the disruption was reported and took the teacher into custody.

    A case has been registered against the accused under section 23 of the Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2000, read with Section 377 and 511 of the IPC. The accused was produced before a court in Saidapet which remanded him in judicial custody."

  • Row Erupts After Exam Paper of Popular Chennai School Terms Protesting Farmers as 'Violent Maniacs'

    A popular CBSE school in Chennai landed in controversy after a question paper termed the farmers who protested against the farm laws during the Republic Day tractor rally as "miscreants" and "violent maniacs." The phrase was used in a politically charged question in a Class 10 exam paper and was framed in ‘letter to an editor’ format, as part of the English Language and Literature paper in the second revision exams held at DAV Boys school, News Minute reported on Friday.

    The exam was held on February 11.

    Part of Section B in the exam paper, the question read: “The diabolical violence that broke out in the national capital on Republic Day filled the hearts of the citizens with condemnation and abhorrence after the farm law protesters went on a rampage destroying public property and attacking police personnel in broad daylight. Write a letter to the Editor of a daily newspaper in your city, condemning such terrible, violent acts of miscreants who fail to realize that country comes before personal needs and gains. Destroying public property, disgracing the national flag, and attacking police personnel are few of the various illegal offences committed, that can never be justified for any reason whatsoever.”
